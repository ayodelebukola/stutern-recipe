Beans is one of the earliest cultivated plants. The oldest findings and proof that we used beans for foods are 9,000 years old and were found in Thailand. Wild varieties of bread beans were gathered in Afghanistan and the Himalayan foothills. Porridge beans is a common food in many Nigerian households and commonly referred to as 'Stewed Beans'. In Yoruba, it is referred to as 'Ewa' or 'Ewa Agoyin'. It is a good dish for vegetarians and in contrast to the common misconceptions about beans being a full proteinaceous dish, it actually has a higher percentage of carbohydrate and also consist of fibers, folate and iron. Some beans however have toxins while they are still raw. Beans used to be very affordable and popular, everyone had access to it, making porridge beans a simple and easy to cook dish without the use of much ingredients. Beans porridge can be made in a variety of ways, usually as a filling, simple 'one-pot' recipe which elevates it to a remarkable status. Porridge beans goes perfectly with the sides: plantains, bread(Nigerian Agege), garri and yam, or whichever side dishes of your choice.

Preparation time: 25 minutes

Cook time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Total time: 1 hour 55 minutes

Serves: 7 people

Ingredients Measurements
Beans (Black-eyed) 5 Cups
Palm Oil 1 Cup
Tomatoes 4 Large
Scotch Bonnet Pepper 6 (for the heat)
Bell Pepper 3 Large
Onion 2 Large 1 medium
Ground Dry Shrimps (Crayfish) Half cup
Seasoning Cubes/Powder 3 Tablespoons
Salt 1 Tablespoon (to taste)

Preparation:

  1. Put the beans in the bowl and use your hands to pick out the dirts in them (if any).
  2. Wash all the peppers and the two large onions then blend them(roughly blended or smoothly) and set aside.
  3. Dice up the medium onion and set aside

How to Cook:

  1. Rinse the beans then put it in a deep pot with 9 cups of hot water.
  2. Add your already diced onions and salt then cover to boil till 80% soft.
  3. This usually takes about 50-55 minutes (but may be more if you are using black eyed beans).
  4. Once your beans is softened, you add the blended pepper and palm oil and stir.
  5. Add your seasoning powder and grounded crayfish stir then then cover.
  6. Taste to confirm if the seasoning is to your taste and if not, add to your satisfaction.
  7. Allow to cook for 25 minutes, so its properly cooked together.
  8. Allow it to cool in the pot for 10 minutes before serving.

    9. YOUR PORRIDGE BEANS IS NOW READY!!!

For better enjoyment: You could fry plantains together with your beans or have them with yam, bread, garri or ay side of your choice.
